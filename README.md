To decode Morse code fluently in your head, this program utilizes two world-renowned learning methods:

â–º 1. The Koch Method (Character Training)
Avoid the "visual bridge" trap (trying to memorize dots and dashes on paper). The Koch method starts with only a few characters (e.g., K and M) at their full target speed. As soon as you reach 90% accuracy, you add another character. Use the checkboxes in the Training Mode to select your current character set!

â–º 2. The Farnsworth Method (The Timing)
The individual characters are played at a high speed (e.g., 20 WPM) so you learn the "melody" of the letter. To give your brain time to process, the spaces between characters and words are artificially lengthened. As you improve, you reduce these spaces until you reach standard timing.

The default values (20 WPM, 550 Hz) are ideal for beginners:

Do not reduce the speed (WPM)! If you lower the WPM too much, you will fall into the habit of "counting dots and dashes," which creates a massive hurdle later on (the "10-WPM wall").

If it feels too fast, do not lower the WPM. Instead, increase the spacing between characters (Farnsworth Principle).

600 Hz corresponds to the standard sidetone of many modern transceivers and is easy on the ears.
